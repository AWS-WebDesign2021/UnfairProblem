Hyacinth Macaw

Andorhynchus Hyacinthinus

Introduction

Hyacinth Macaws, also known as Andorhynchus Hyacinthinus, are stunning blue and yellow parrots. These birds
are known to be a meter in length, have a mass of 2.6-3.7 lbs, and can live up to 50 years old in the wild. Although this species
is large in size and mass, the estimated population of this macaw has decreased over many decades. With the organization
of foundations dedicated to the preservation of this beautiful creature, it has been proven successful in keeping these animals alive.
Now, there are more than double the amount of Hyacinth Macaws in the world since the 1980s.


Regions

The hyacinth macaw is mostly located in Central and South America. The biggest regions where these
parrots can be found are in:

  1. Brazil
  2. Bolivia
  3. Paraguay

Since these macaws are completely dependent on fruits, seeds, and nuts, their main habitat is the South American tropical
rainforest, where many wildlife resides.

Make A Difference

Since the realization of the total population of these unique macaws (an estimate of 450 total in the 1980s),
many organizations were founded in order to create a difference and repopulate the world with these beautiful
creatures. Luckily, these efforts were successful, and by the 2000s there were more than 3000 Hyacinth Macaws in
the world. Although not as populous as they were centuries prior, the macaw has made a comeback and will hopefully
continue to grow its numbers. If you want to join one of these organizations or was to find out more ways to donate, go to the
following websites to find out more:
